Chrondle × Misty Step comic-ops — aesthetic decision lab

Card chrondle-eng-ui-tokens (absorbing backlog.d/017). Question: does Chrondle adopt the Misty Step comic-ops aesthetic (any flavor), or keep its bespoke archival token system? Each option mocks the classic game surface (masthead, hint card, guess input, guess history). Options are structurally distinct directions, not shade variations.

Verdict Winner: Option 1 — no-adopt (bespoke archival tokens)

Decision: Chrondle does NOT adopt the Misty Step comic-ops aesthetic on any product surface. The bespoke archival token system (Outfit/Karla, 4px radius, paper metaphor, feedback-* semantic tokens) remains the design system of record, now fully consolidated by the token sweep shipped with this card.

Rationale:

  • Puzzle-clarity risk (options 2, 5, 8): halftone fields, ink outlines, and offset shadows add visual noise exactly where the game demands fast scanning of year digits and EARLIER/LATER direction. Rotated Impact-style display type actively fights the tabular-numeral discipline the guess history depends on.
  • Genre trust: Chrondle is a daily Wordle-genre game with paying subscribers. The quiet NYT-adjacent register is a conversion asset; a comic register reads as a different (younger, jokier) product and repositions the brand without a product reason.
  • Identity coherence beats fleet coherence here: comic-ops is Misty Step's internal ops voice. Chrondle is a consumer product with its own established identity; forcing the fleet skin onto it is brand centralization without user benefit.
  • Chrome-only and moments-only flavors (3, 4) are the worst of both: two competing registers on one screen; the win-moment "POW" (option 4) is charming once and grating on day 30 of a streak.
  • Newsprint (6) is the strongest challenger — it is history-native and clarity-preserving — but it is a full bespoke rebrand, out of scope and not obviously better than the shipped archival direction it largely duplicates.
  • Share card (7) is the only surface where comic-ops earns anything (loud, social, outside the play loop), noted as a possible future standalone ticket — not shipped here, and not enough to justify adopting the system.

Shipped direction: the semantic-token sweep, registry/kit split, and vendor-boundary centralization in this card ARE the implementation of the winning direction: Chrondle's bespoke tokens, made rigorous. Backlog item 017's oracle is satisfied as a deliberate no-adoption decision with rendered option set (this lab).
