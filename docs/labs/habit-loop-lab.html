Chrondle habit-loop lab — entry & completion

Card chrondle-ux-habit-loop. Two decisions: (1) what a logged-out first-timer sees at / so they can state what the game is and start in one tap, while a returning visitor reaches their mode in zero taps; (2) what the completion screen does at the moment of maximal engagement to bring the player back tomorrow. 8 structurally distinct entry options (E1–E8) + 8 completion options (C1–C8). Constraint from VISION: no retention surface may leak era/year; no dark patterns. All mocks are wireframes — mode colors match the live token palette.

Verdict (delegated mode — operator may re-verdict)

Entry: E2 — pitch + how-it-works strip, plus cookie redirect for returning visitors. E2 is the only option that answers all three first-timer questions (what / how / why return) inside one viewport without demoting the mode gallery (E3, E6), adding dismissal state (E5, E8), or creating an integrity foot-gun (E4's canned specimen). E7's dashboard is the most seductive loser: with the returning-visitor redirect in place, home is fundamentally a first-touch surface, and E7 optimizes it for the audience that never lands there — its checklist idea is delivered where finished players actually are, via C3.

Completion: C2 — "come back tomorrow" card replacing the passive countdown, plus C3 — today-state checklist in KEEP PLAYING. C2 converts the countdown from dead time into an explicit next-day contract with the streak as stake, and carries the measurable reminder opt-in (calendar file: works logged-out, zero permission friction, honest). C4 buries the answer reveal (the game's actual climax), C6/C7 are nag-shaped, C8 requires persistence that doesn't exist, C5 is a visual overhaul out of scope, C1 under-delivers the moment. C3 is kept because the card's plan item 3 names it and it shares all its plumbing with C2.
